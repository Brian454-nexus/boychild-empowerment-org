Our Vision, Mission, Motto & Objectives

"It's easier to build strong children than to repair broken men."
— Frederick Douglass

VISION

To enlighten the society and create awareness on issues surrounding the wellbeing and development of the boy child

MISSION

Create a safe place that promotes mentorship, empowerment and formation of the boy child to facilitate a smooth transition into responsible men.

MOTTO

Transforming boys into responsible men

OBJECTIVE

Promoting gender equality, adressing learning disparities and providing mentorship opportunities.

FAQS

Have Any Questions?

BCEK primarily supports boys aged 13 to 17 and young men aged 18 to 35, recognizing these as critical phases in emotional, physical, and social development. Additionally, adult men are key contributors as mentors and role models, while girls and women offer essential support and diverse perspectives that enrich the boys' development journey.

BCEK began its transformative journey in Trans-Nzoia County, with a vision to expand its impact across Kenya and ultimately throughout Africa. The organization is committed to reshaping the narrative around the boychild by addressing challenges at the grassroots and scaling sustainable empowerment across the continent.

    WORD FROM THE FOUNDER

    " The boychild does not exist in isolation, nor can he thrive alone. He is engulfed in a sea of conflicting voices, shifting social norms, and emotional turbulence in today's volatile, uncertain, complex, and ambiguous (VUCA) world. Technology is just one of the many forces shaping — and often shaking — his developing identity. But amid this chaos, who listens to him? Who understands his silent cries, his unspoken questions, and his unmet emotional needs?

    With no safe space to vent, no mentor to confide in, and no guide to help navigate his inner world, his frustrations fester into dangerous outcomes — substance abuse, violence, crime, and indulgence in unrestrained desires. We at Boychild Empowerment Kenya (BCEK) urge society to wake up, take notice, and stand in the gap. The boychild's future is not just his — it's our collective responsibility.

    ~Sir Stanley Barasa Siakilo, Founder of BCEK

    Boychild Empowerment Kenya (BCEK) is committed to restoring dignity, opportunity, and voice to the boychild through mentorship, advocacy, and holistic support.
