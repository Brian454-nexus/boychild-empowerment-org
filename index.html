BCEK is a humanitarian, non-political, non-religious, non-profit and legally registered organization that is committed to pursue the welfare, social justice and social advancement of the boychild; and to enlighten the community on the need to give equal opportunities to all children irrespective of their sex.

Our Vision, Mission, Motto & Objectives

"It's easier to build strong children than to repair broken men."
— Frederick Douglass

VISION

To enlighten the society and create awareness on issues surrounding the wellbeing and development of the boy child

MISSION

Create a safe place that promotes mentorship, empowerment and formation of the boy child to facilitate a smooth transition into responsible men.

MOTTO

Transforming boys into responsible men

OBJECTIVE

Promoting gender equality, addressing learning disparities and providing mentorship opportunities.

FAQS

Have Any Questions?

BCEK primarily supports boys aged 13 to 17 and young men aged 18 to 35, recognizing these as critical phases in emotional, physical, and social development. Additionally, adult men are key contributors as mentors and role models, while girls and women offer essential support and diverse perspectives development journey.

BCEK began its transformative journey in Trans-Nzoia County, with a vision to expand its impact across Kenya and ultimately throughout Africa. The organization is committed to reshaping the narrative around the boychild by addressing challenges at the grassroots and scaling sustainable empowerment across the continent.

    WORD FROM THE FOUNDER

    As the founder and director of Boy Child Empowerment Kenya, I call upon all and sundry, parents, teachers, civil society organizations, all levels of government, and other stakeholders in Kenya and Africa space, to lend a voice against the growing neglect of the boy child, and to create more platforms and resources that will promote the interests and total wellbeing of the boy child, and salvage their fast waning posture and position in the society.

    ~Sir Stanley Barasa Siakilo, Founder of BCEK
